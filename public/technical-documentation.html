Introduction

Adding a fitness routine into your lifestyle can greatly improve your health and quality of life. The benefits to your heart, muscular system and mind are many. Let's learn how to get started with fitness and exercise.

Getting Started

The best approach to getting started with exercise is to get into a routine that you can follow and incorporate around your current lifestyle. Make sure you set health and fitness goals that are realistic and achieveable.

You will need to consider factors such as work, family and other commitments before you decide on when and what you will spend your time on in the gym. Pick a time that works well consistently for your schedule.

Another important factor to consider is exercise intensity. Many people start exercising at intensities too high for them to maintain, making them feel unmotivated and exercise feel too hard.

  • Scheduling your exercise time around your current schedule and picking an exercise routine that aligns with your goals without taking up too much of your time.
  • Start an exercise program that is at a manageable intensity. You want a program that you can stick to for a long period of time.

Set your goals using SMART goal setting:

  • Specific
  • Measureable
  • Achievable
  • Relevant
  • Time-bound

Resistance Training

Resistance training is an essential part of any fitness routine. Resistance training aids in musclular strength and hypertrophy (muscle growth). Resistance training is performed by stressing the muscular system under load (aka resistance).

Strength can be built by performing exercises in line with your goals under a heavy load with few repitions, typically in the 1 to 5 repitition range. For the best results, compund exercises should be used while building strength like this. The load should not be so heavy that it compromises form. Exercises are clustered into sets with 2 to five minutes rest in between. Resistance training like this is not optimal for building muscle.

Muscle building works best with a light to moderate load placed on the muscle. For best results it is recommended to focus on a mix of isolation exercises and compund movements. Repitions are higher for building muscle. 6 to 12 repititions are great for building a mix of muscle and stength. 12 repitions and up are great for building muscle endurance.

Building muscle and strength is essential for preventing injury and maintaining balance and coordination as you age.

Resistance training can be divided into many different training splits. Three to four days a week for resistance training is more than enough to provide the necessary adaptations to help you reach your goals.

It is recommended to find a good coach before starting out with compound lifting, to ensure safety and proper form.

  • Squat
  • Deadlift
  • Bench Press
  • Overhead Press
Cardiovascular Training

Cardiovascular training increases the effeciency of the heart, lungs and vascular system (the network of blood vessels throughout the body).

For the best health and fitness results a mix of low and high intensity cardio training should be incorporated into your program. Majority of your training should be spent doing zone two cardio, while the higher intensity zone 5 cardio should be reserved for short bursts at the end of your work out. Zone 2 and zone 5 refers to different heart rate zones.

  • Low intensity workouts that raise the heart rate and can be maintained for a long period of time.
  • Breathing should be steady and you should be able to uncomfortably maintain a conversation during your training session.
  • Increases the hearts maximum capacity to fill and pump blood, making the heart more efficient.
  • Walking/Hiking
  • Jogging
  • Cycling
  • Rowing
  • High intensity training reserved for the end of a training session.
  • Breathing should be hard and uncomfortable. Aim for just below the threshold of maximum effort.
  • Roughly only 5 minutes of zone 5 cardio three times per week is needed for good results. Sprinting intervals or tabata are great options for zone 5 cardio.
  • Zone 5 cardio increases the strength and force of the muscle contractions that the heart uses to pump blood. Zone 2 cardio should be your first priority, using zone 5 to make for a well rounded cardiovascular program.
Recovery

Recovery is just as important as the exercise stimulus. Exercise is voluntary stress on your body to achieve certain fitness adaptations and goals. Recovery is an essential part of the process as these adaptations can only really grow during rest.

Sleep, diet and stress management are all essential parts of the recovery process.
Body Recomposition

Body recomposition refers to weight loss and muscle gain. Both of these require you to take a look at your nutition.

Weight loss does not necessarily require an exercise stimulus. The most important factor if your goal is losing weight is to maintain a caloric deficit. Tracking your food and your calories will help keep you on track and ensure your success. Check out MyFitnessPal or LoseIt! for great calorie tracking apps and recourses.

Building muscle will definitely need you to spend time in the gym. Resistance training paired with a high protein diet and a caloric surplus will give you the right stimulus to grow. Aim for 2g of protein per kilogram of bodyweight per day and use a calorie tracker to track your weight gain and progress.
